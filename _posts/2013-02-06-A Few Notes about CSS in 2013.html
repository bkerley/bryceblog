--- kind: regular layout: post title: "A Few Notes about CSS in 2013" date: 2013-02-06T02:50:49+00:00 --- {% raw %}
  • Web fonts are nice to have, most of your viewers should be able to see things mostly the same way you designed them.
  • It’s cool to not care about Internet Explorer. It’s even cooler knowing that it’ll get things mostly right.
  • Media queries: if you’re designing for smartphones and tablets, you can take a few steps to make the page a bit more usable. If you’re designing for overhead projectors universities bought a decade ago, you can take huge steps to re-layout a normal 960px page.
  • Making your site work on a narrow projector is easy when you can swap out your standard font for a condensed font.
  • SASS is still the best.
