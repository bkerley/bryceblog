  <link-description>&lt;p&gt;Ruby doesn&amp;rsquo;t support that particular technique because it&amp;rsquo;s object-oriented more than it&amp;rsquo;s functional:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;pre class="prettyprint"&gt; 
[1, 2, 3].each{|n| puts n}
&lt;/pre&gt;
&lt;p&gt;However, in 1.9.2, you can snatch a &lt;code&gt;Method&lt;/code&gt; from an object using the &lt;code&gt;Object#method&lt;/code&gt; method:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;pre class="prettyprint"&gt; 
method(:print) #=&amp;gt; #&amp;lt;Method: Object(Kernel)#print&amp;gt;
5.method(:+).call(8) #=&amp;gt; 13
&lt;/pre&gt;
&lt;p&gt;And you can even turn a &lt;code&gt;Method&lt;/code&gt; into a &lt;code&gt;Proc&lt;/code&gt; or syntactic block:&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;pre class="prettyprint"&gt; 
"big balls".method(:split).to_proc #=&amp;gt; #&amp;lt;Proc:0x00000100d98ec8 (lambda)&amp;gt;
[1, 2, 3].each(&amp;amp;method(:puts))
&lt;/pre&gt;</link-description>
